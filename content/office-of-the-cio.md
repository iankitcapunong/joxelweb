# Office of the CIO
With the changing dynamics of healthcare, the role of a Chief Information Officer (CIO) within a healthcare organization has increased significantly. A CIO now has to be well versed with the trends within healthcare to ensure the strategic direction of the organization is being maintained, has to make sure the system and network architecture within the organization is sustainable from both a short-term and long-term perspective, and make sure the information architecture is aligned to provide insights into population health management. Joxel provides the “Office of the CIO” role to ensure organizations can focus on their core competency of managing their clinical and financial operations, while the CIO focuses on ensuring IT is being leveraged to sustain that growth.
